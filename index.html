Day One routes

Three locks, any order, all three required

Day One constants

  • First draft is always Bedroom, Closet or Hallway, rotation is not fixed.
  • Trunk in the Bedroom. Cloister is empty. Dinner is always Fish (Aquarium → +10).
  • You start with 0 stars. Observatory earns 1 coin. Telescope isn't in the item pool if you don't start the day with stars.
  • First-time-per-save, not day-one-specific: Security must be drafted from its centre door; the Garage trunk holds an Upgrade Disk.

The three locks

  1. Open an Antechamber door, any one of five rooms with an E/W/S lever.
  2. Reach Underground Cartside, Tomb, or Pump Room.
  3. Reach Underground Gearside, Pump Room, Foundation, or Power Hammer.
The hard constraint: Pump Room can't do both sides of the Underground unless it's powered by a Boiler Room. There isn't enough tank capacity to drain the Fountain and drop the Reservoir at once without the reserve tank.

  2. Burning Glass + 3 Microchips → Satellite Dish

Outer room: Tomb or Trading Post

Open the Outer Room as soon as possible, then look for Tomb or Trading Post, 63.59% to see one first round, 52.01% if a Greenhouse is active.

Target 1st round 1st + 2nd round
Tomb 34.73% 68.46%
Trading Post 39.16% 77.18%
Either one 63.59% 95.82%
With 2 dice you get either, 100%. With 1 die, 94.76% to see at least one, provided no Archives or Greenhouse. If you see one while holding dice, just take it rather than rerolling to force the other.

Tomb, simpler, faster, cheaper on steps. Fewer keys overall means less agency, but you only need to hit one of three win conditions (Power Hammer, Pool, Foundation). Covers games with good shops, or where you already have the Secret Garden Key.

Trading Post, higher overall consistency (multiple shots at Silver Key, Secret Garden Key, Battery) but expensive step-wise, since it needs repeated backtracks. Wants a good exit from high ranks via the Garage. Best when low on keys.

If you miss both: Toolshed (immediate resource keeps runs alive) → Hovel (specifically for low-gem runs, but watch that you can't draft step-cost rooms like Nursery when too low) → Shelter (best for Boiler Room strats; lets you take Archives and Weight Room without punishment, and there's 1 gem in the safe).

Speed

Glitchless inheritance runs

The route

Secret Garden / Great Hall  +  Pool  +  Tomb

Great Hall has the higher ceiling; Secret Garden isn't slow and is preferred for Dare/Curse. Tomb + Pool skips the Basement entirely, which is where the time is.

Collect in order

  • Picking up a unique special item for the first time each day plays an animation. Grab resources first (key, coin, gem), specials after.
  • Or kill the delay by quickly opening and closing the Blueprints.

Items to ignore

  • Upgrade Disks, Magnifying Glass, Vault Keys, Car Keys.
  • Leave Workshop spawns (e.g. Battery Pack) on the table until you need to craft.
  • Worth it: Running Shoes (movement, especially outside), Compass (fewer rooms needed to build a path).

Memorised solves

  • Safes: 415, 1225, 812, most accept DD/MM and MM/DD both.
  • Parlor solutions.
  • Pump Room reservoir boat: fill tank 1 from Greenhouse, then from Reservoir.
  • Gear puzzle: rotate the first gear 4 times for the fastest line to 46. Grab Mechanarium on the way back from Underpass if you need it.

Dartboard optimisation

The sides of the board have no collision hitbox, collect keys through the sides before the board finishes opening.

On Day One the dartboard is always 6 questions in the same pattern:

single → addition → subtraction → subtraction → multiplication → subtraction & multiplication

Answers are limited, so pre-position the cursor. Multiplication tends to give 12 or 20, near the top.

Day One checklist

Draft engine

What the game does when it shows you three doors

Draft pools are per-doorway

Not one pool. Every doorway has four (Common Free, Rare Free, Common Gem, Rare Gem), combined at draft time. The names only loosely match what's inside.

  • Normal drafting uses the max of the four sizes; other draft types sum them.
  • Drafting removes one copy from every pool containing it. The pool with fewest copies empties first.
  • Duplicate hotspots: north into B9/D9 (Common Gem = Rare Gem), south into B1/D1 (Common Free = Common Gem). Both distort Silver/Prism behaviour.
  • Chamber of Mirrors rooms aren't removed on draft, unless already on the estate.

Day One Bequest has the smallest pool in the game, no Found Floorplans, no Studio Additions unlocked. The more rooms you draft in a day, the easier the drained pool makes finding a specific one.

  • A higher-priority room blocks lower ones just by being in the pool, even if its own conditions don't apply.
  • Already offered in slots 1–2? The roll still happens, then the draw fails anyway.
  • A failed roll does not retry on redraws, but does retry at a new location.
  • If a temporary restriction bars the room before the random chance is rolled, it can still try again on a redraw.
  • A Weighted Room in Slot 3 is ignored by validation, so it will not be sorted into an earlier slot even if it is cheaper.
  • Never appear from a Secret Passage, Prism Key, Silver Key or Berry. They do still appear from a Cloister of Draxus, because the Cloister only acts during validation.
  • Garage and Utility Closet check the previous two rooms aren't both Dead Ends, only if Slot 2 was a normal draw.

Priority filters

Each floorplan rolls to be accepted; the draw proceeds using only accepted ones. First attempt only. Multiple activations of the same filter in one day don't stack.

Rarity still technically matters, but the pool gets so pinched that rarity effects become negligible.

Normal drafting, filters, and the rarity gate

Free vs gem

  • Slot 1 is always a free draw.
  • Days 1–3: first 6 − day drafts are all-free. V-Mode → just the first 2.
  • Slot 2 rolls for gem off rank and gems held. If it hits, Slot 3 becomes gem too. If not, Slot 3 rolls separately. Live numbers → Odds desk.
You see slightly more gem rooms holding 1 or 4 gems than at other counts, the table isn't monotonic. Being at 0 gems means you stop picking between 3 real options.

Filters

  • Discard filter, drawn by normal drafting → out until its deck reshuffles. Rerolled rooms won't be offered again immediately.
  • Runback filter, first round of a draft, rooms from the previous draft's last round may be filtered. Rolled once for the whole round:
Rarity Filtered out
Commonplace 60%
Standard 80%
Unusual 90%
Rare 99%
  • Doesn't apply to the first draft of the day.
  • Secret Passage sets no filter, next draft starts clean. Prism Key does set it.
  • Secret Garden Key, Key 8, and Berry Picker each run a hidden round that counts as "last round".
  • West Path ignores it and doesn't even clear it.

The deck-size gate

Rarity is chosen first (day × rank × slot × Solarium), then the deck is size-checked:

Group Required size, Com / Std / Unu / Rare
Any free deck 3 / 3 / 3 / 3
Gem, V-Mode, post-46, or day 16+ 5 / 5 / 4 / 4
Gem, day 8+ 4 / 4 / 3 / 3
Gem, otherwise 4 / 3 / 3 / 3

Too small → mark it, climb Commonplace → Standard → Unusual → Rare until one fits. Then pick uniformly at random among all marked decks with size ≥ 1.

This is the whole game of rare-hunting. The game expends lower-rarity rooms until ~3 remain before offering higher rarities. Emptying commons doesn't boost rares, it stops commons being eligible, and everything left is equally weighted.

Draw attempts

  1. Everything above.
  2. Same, minus priority filters. Fails outright if no deck is large enough.
  3. Shuffle the four decks (clearing the discard filter), repeat attempt 2 with the same rarity pick. Can't fail the size check.
  4. Draws a Closet. Unconditional. An inexplicable Closet means nothing passed filters.

The first attempt almost never finds a deck large enough, even with a colour filter on, which flattens the odds across every rarity that has passing floorplans.

Validation

Runs after all three draws, covering slots up to the last normal draw.

  1. Duplicates → earliest one rerolls as an Any Draw (with priority filter).
  2. Three Dead Ends → Slot 2 rerolls, dead ends banned. Exceptions: Cloister of Draxus, niche duct situations, and corners when you're out of 2-way bends.
  3. Cloister of Draxus → inverted: non-dead-ends reroll into dead ends.
  4. Slot 1 came out gem → reroll as a free draw. The first room always costs 0; if it would cost, the cost is bypassed.
  5. Sort by gem cost ascending, then stated rarity (most common first).
The other drafting procedures

Library

  • Filters certain rooms out, notably you cannot find Furnace or Maid's Chamber.
  • At 0 gems it offers two free rooms; otherwise two gem rooms. Mind this if you're hunting Study.
  • Pulls from mostly Unusual and Rare, roughly 50:50.
  • The only consistent way to find Foundation / Study / Attic on Day One.

Silver Key

  • Ignores rarity. Tries 3-way or 4-way first, then 2-ways.
  • Draft to an edge to force 3-ways.
  • Pool is pinched: no dead ends, many 2-ways unavailable, and Foundation is removed. Locker Room counts as a 3-way. Gallery and Trophy Room are in the 2-way pool even on Day One.
  • Prefers gemless rooms unless you hold 4+ gems.
  • Empty silver pool → falls back to mostly normal drafting, gemless-first.
  • Does not consume the Garage forced draw.
Silver Key on an edge is the only consistent Pool finder. Hold 4 gems so Slot 2 can be a gem draft, and expend your other gem 3-ways first (Drawing Room, Security, Boiler, Courtyard, Aquarium).

Prism Key / Secret Passage

  • Prism drafting is jank. Known consequences:
  • Can draft rooms not yet added to the pool, Morning Room, Casino, Solarium.
  • Pulling green very likely gives a Slot 1 cost-bypassed gem room, since most greens cost gems.
  • Can duplicate rooms already in the mansion if the pool is pinched.
  • Can put 4-ways at edges and 3-ways at corners.
  • Prism on an Aquarium → reselect colour by hovering in and out.

Dynamic rarity

The rarity that actually matters

Every room has an internal rarity separate from the one printed on the card. Most rooms are more common than they claim. Security, Courtyard and West Wing Hall are all Dynamic Common despite saying Standard.

The first week, room by room

Empty cells mean no Dynamic Rarity is set, so the room falls back to its Base Rarity. Every one of these first-week changes is skipped entirely once you reach Room 46, or if V Mode is active.

How to read the pool

  • Commons are unavoidable. Approach Day One expecting to draft every single one eventually.
  • When commons are spent, Standards appear at mid ranks. Standards hold multiple 3-ways, Gym, Chapel, Darkroom, Dining Room. This is when you branch liberally.
  • Unusuals are infrequent, and hold the win conditions. Take them when you see the right ones.
  • Gem Unusuals are much harder to see than Free Unusuals, you'll have emptied the free pool long before the gem pool.
  • Do not plan around seeing any Rares.
Day One's two hardest rooms are Pool (Unusual) and Study (Rare), and Veteran Mode fixes neither. Everything else it resets.

What the shape of the pool does to your run

Impact 1, dead ends front-load

The Free Common pool is full of dead ends. The Free Standard pool is full of 3-ways. So the early game tests you on placing dead ends wisely without wasting doors; the midgame branches so freely that runs survive some waste.

Impact 2, gem economy is negative

More rooms take gems (Passageway, Security, Kitchen) than the free pool produces (Den, Parlor, Guest Bedroom). Hence: be picky about gem rooms, and Commissary sets that sell gems are much stronger than sets that don't.

Circumventing rarity entirely

  1. Duct draws, uniform, ignores rarity.
  2. Forced draws, ignores rarity.
  3. Silver Key, ignores rarity.
  4. Priority filters, pinches the pool until rarity is negligible.
  5. Prism Key / Secret Passage, reaches outside the pool entirely.
  6. Emptying commons, the gate does the rest.

Day One Upgrade Disks

  • Disks don't affect rooms you've already drafted, except the Nursery. If you already have a Nursery, the +8 steps upgrade improves its +5 to +8; the other two upgrades delete your drafted Nursery's ability. Don't take them.
  • Since 1.7, Day One Veteran disk logic avoids rooms you've already drafted, from the second disk onward, at 70%.
  • Consequence: Aquarium, Mail Room, and Cloister get upgraded disproportionately, because they're rarely drafted early.
  • Disks trade at Trading Post as a tier 4 item, the tier that includes the Secret Garden Key.
Don't spend your first Upgrade Disk until you've seen the Outer Room, unless you found it very early. As a tier-4 trade chip it may be worth more than any upgrade.

Doors & area

The theory that decides runs

If you take one thing from this page: DON'T WASTE DOORS EARLY. A door wasted on draft 3 means more dead ends in every one of the 30+ drafts remaining.

Terminology

Term Meaning
Draft Selecting an open door and adding a room
Round One offer of 3 rooms. A draft can have several rounds if you reroll
Open Door A door pointing at an empty slot. You need ≥1 to continue; you want ≥2
Draftable Area Rooms reachable from ONE open door, assuming you don't immediately draft a dead end. Cone-shaped
Effective Area Draftable Area that doesn't overlap another open door's. The real measure of a door's worth
Path Dependency An empty slot reachable from only one open door
Chokepoint A slot that must take a specific orientation to reach the destination
Wasted Door A door pointed at a wall (1 wasted) or at another open door (2 wasted, but makes a shortcut)
ADA Available Door Average, mean doors of rooms left in the pool. Typically 2.0–2.5
EDA Effective Door Average, mean doors you actually see from a given open door. Weighted by rarity, special drafting, and where the door points
ADA of 2.0 means: place every room perfectly without wasting a door and you still gain no new open doors. Every bend consumes one and replaces it. More 3/4-ways drafted → ADA falls. More dead ends drafted → ADA rises.

The spiral of dead ends

  1. Waste a door early → EDA drops for the rest of the day
  2. Lower EDA → fewer path options
  3. Fewer options → less likely to find the direction you want
  4. When you do find it, it often costs a wasted door
  5. → back to step 1

Wasting Commonplace doors hurts most, those rooms were coming anyway, and you spent their doors on nothing. Wasting a Rare's door barely registers. Wasting late barely registers either, because there are few drafts left to punish you.

Backtracking

Travel costs 1 step per room. There and back costs twice. Entrance Hall → Passageway = 8 steps. Entrance Hall → Passageway → back to use open doors → Passageway again = 24.

  • Finish what a room offers so you never return, or place it close to your branch.
  • Bunch backtrack rooms together so one trip solves several goals.
  • Place shops in later ranks unless you're already rich.
  • Open early trunks when you have no hammer; speculate on later trunks, they're closer to your backtrack path.

Reasons to backtrack, worth tracking: trunk at Den/Bedroom/Courtyard · shop items at Kitchen/Commissary/Trading Post · dinner at Dining Room · dig spots at green rooms and occasionally Storeroom/Tomb · items left at the Workshop · coin at the Pantry while fishing for the Coin Purse · unsolved dartboard while fishing for a Keycard. Also peek into nearby rooms to note which doors are Security doors.

Two items with special mention

Compass

  • Massively biases rotation north. Very predictable → great for reducing wasted doors.
  • Cost: backfilling ranks becomes super unlikely. Kiss the area behind you goodbye unless you find a 4-way to point south.
  • Because higher ranks are more likely to be locked, opening the North door saves keys long-term.

Battery Pack

  • Picking it up may make the Workshop less likely, specifically on Day One Veteran, 50%.
  • So be careful grabbing a Battery before you have the Workshop.

Intermission: the centre Closet

The Entrance Hall's west door almost always reaches B2, and its east door almost always reaches D2, the manor walls redirect the paths. But the north door's Effective Area is tiny. So put a dead end or a straight there. That's the case for the centre Closet opening.

There's a companion trick at B1/D1: because the game can't normally offer three dead ends, the Draftable Area of the west door to A1 always contains A2. So if you draft a 3-way at B1 or D1, explore the north door first, then you know whether B2 paths to A2. If it does, put a dead end at A1. If it doesn't, put a bend at A1 and reach A2 from there.

Economy

Gems, keys, steps, items

Gems

The pool is gem-negative by design. More rooms take than give.

At low gems

  • Draft toward edges / corners / closed paths to place Dead Ends, they're very often gem-positive.
  • Accept Red Rooms. Multi-door, no gem cost (except Aquarium). Ideal for carving a path with nothing.
  • Keep one gem as far as possible.
  • Skip Garage/Security if it keeps you above 0, even though they're good rooms.
Many gem rooms refund part or all of their cost. That's the major reason not to sit at 0.

Red Rooms, honestly

Friends when low on keys or gems. Fiends when high on both, they throw a wrench into a consistent gameplan. Many have Security doors: embrace or avoid depending on your access.

Keys

Keys are your room budget. Doors start locking at Rank 4. Higher ranks lock more.

Lock randomness is controlled, you're very unlikely to get too many locked or unlocked doors in a row.

At low keys

  • Lean west wing to find the Garage.
  • Avoid opening trunks with keys.
  • Branch out, avoid open doors with small Effective Areas.
  • At mid ranks (30–60% locked), point multiple open doors at the same empty slot. Wastes doors, buys more chances at an unlocked one.
  • Have Security access? Prefer rooms that can have Security doors.

Key sources

Locksmith · Powered Furnace · Laundry · Servant's Quarters · Music Room · Locker Room · Walk-In Closet · Lockpick set · Metal Detector · lab experiment

Rooms at lower ranks are less likely to be locked or Security. That's what makes them excellent for building resources before you push deep.

Items

Type Examples Implication
1 · Single-use Sledgehammer, Sleeping Mask, Shovel Benefits from backtracking. Shapes where you place rooms.
2 · Passive Running Shoes, Lockpick, Metal Detector, Compass Take on sight.
3 · Special keys Prism Key, Silver Key, Secret Garden Key Behave more like keys than items.

Item blocking, the rule that shapes shops

A room will not spawn a special item that already exists in the mansion or is used in a contraption. Have a Shovel from the Courtyard? You won't find one at the Commissary or Workshop.

  • Items offered in Commissary/Trading Post don't count as existing, new rooms can still spawn them.
  • Items traded away at Trading Post go back into the pool.
  • Silver Key and Prism Key return to the pool once used. Secret Garden Key does not, it's single-use.

Item hoarding

  • Workshop always spawns a contraption component. Draft it late to fish for your last Power Hammer piece.
  • Commissary avoids spawning items you already have, use this to force it to sell gems.
  • Trading Post, the more you hold, the more pinched its offer pool. Hoard; hoarding multiples of the same tier works exceptionally well.
  • Billiard Room, solve the dartboard after you've fished for a Keycard, so the 20% Keycard doesn't default into 2 keys.

Commissary sets

The Commissary offers 1 of 7 sets, and only if you have none of the items in it. Invalid → the next set down the list is chosen.

You hold Chance the set sells gems
Nothing 4 / 7
Only Shovel or only Hammer 2 / 7
Both Shovel and Hammer 7 / 7, guaranteed
Sleeping Mask (best single item) 6 / 7
If none of the 7 sets are valid, Commissary always offers Gem, Key and Upgrade Disk (plus Shovel if possible). You can peek what it's selling from one of its doors, but not the other.

Infinites

Nurse's Station infinite: Nurse's Station (free movement in a 10–11 step radius) + Powered Furnace (steps → keys) + Powered Laundry (keys → dice, gems, gold, steps) = infinite steps, gems, keys and dice. Slow, but infinite.

Others: Casino for infinite coins. Bedroom adjacent to another Bedroom, or to an opened Antechamber, for infinite steps.

Boiler Room dice laundering (allows Lab rerolling tech): Boiler Room → duct into a connector → from the connector, duct into edges to drop Lab / Laundry / Furnace → Lab-reroll to "set to 40 steps" with any good trigger → powered Lab to blackbridge into the Office for starting coin → print keys at the Furnace with your steps → trade keys into dice at the Laundry. Swap steps↔gems where useful.

Security doors

Rank 3 onward

From Rank 3, doors can be Security doors. They get more likely at higher ranks. You need a Keycard, or a Utility Closet + Security room.

Setting Limit Extra effect
Low 3 -
Medium 4 -
High 6 Makes doors more likely to be Security

The limit is where new rooms stop generating Security doors. It doesn't count doors made by the Closed Exhibit.

Playing around access

  • Keycard's earliest sources: Closet, Guest Bedroom, Billiard Room, trunks.
  • Miss all of those → draft Utility Closet and Security earlier.
  • Down to one open door? Avoid 2-door rooms that can have Security doors, Workshop, Commissary, Lab, Patio at certain orientations. That's a run-ender.
  • Have Security but no Utility Closet? Don't set High immediately.
Passageway at High security is bugged and may have Security doors below rank 3.

Not entering rooms

  • Room with a Security door you can't open → don't enter.
  • Workshop with a Battery/Lever you can't use yet → don't enter.
  • Garage drafted from the North door → the 3 keys' hitbox sits slightly outside; take them from the room to the south without entering. Saves 2 steps.
  • Peeking is free intel: whether Office/Secret Garden spread coin or fruit; what the Commissary sells (from one door only).

The one draft that ignores the draft pool

The exact ordering algorithm

All 8 in a list, manipulated in this order. You see slots 1–3; a redraw advances to the next three, cyclically. Positions 9–16 repeat 1–8, useful with Archives.

  1. Shuffle uniformly. One room picked at random at a time until all 8 are placed, no bias.
  2. Rare Room Penalty. Tomb, then Schoolhouse, then Shrine each roll to be shoved to the back. See the chart above for the three rate tiers.
  3. First time ever drafting an Outer Room: Root Cellar, Toolshed, Hovel forced to slots 1–3. Disabled if it's day 1 and V-Mode came from fast drafting.
  4. Whatever you drafted last time: if it's in the front three, shoved to slot 4.
  5. Colour filters, each moving its room to slot 1, applied in this sequence: Violet moves Hovel, Green moves Root Cellar, Yellow moves Trading Post. All three active leaves you looking at Trading Post, Root Cellar, Hovel. A Greenhouse activates Green, so it actively pushes Root Cellar at you.
  6. Blue Color Filter active, 50/50 between two branches:
    • Branch A: Toolshed, Shelter, Shrine to slots 1, 2, 3. Then a 50% chance Schoolhouse jumps to slot 1.
    • Branch B: Shrine, Shelter, Schoolhouse to slots 1, 2, 3. Then a 50% chance Toolshed jumps to slot 1.
  7. Draxus active, or Blackprints common → Tomb to slot 1.
Speedrun read: step 7 is last, so it overrides everything. On a Blackprint-common day, or with Draxus active, Tomb is slot 1 for free and you keep your dice.

Blessing of the Monk inserts its room at position 3, displacing what was there to the redraw. The monked room never reappears on redraws.

Monk / Berry, dragging manor rooms outside
  • Monk or Berry let you draft any grounds room out here, including Antechamber, Foundation, Room 46, Entrance Hall.
  • They keep every property except one: they become Dead Ends.
  • Non-permanent rooms pulled this way are removed from the main pool for the day.
  • Not reversible: already drafted your only copy inside? You can still take it outside, the Outer Room acts as if a second unused copy exists.
  • Room 8: Monk + call it a day in Room 8 → draftable as an Outer Room next day, no Key 8 needed. Never in the pool, so Monk never removes it.
West Path notes
  • Access via the Garage door, or unlatch the West Gate from inside, permanent once opened.
  • Cost from the Entrance Hall at day start: 4 steps round trip, 6 if you enter.
  • Redraw effects work out here. Pool-modifying effects (Pool, Repellent) do nothing.
  • Wall-clipping: rooms on the west wing south of the Garage can be used through the wall, two Locker Room lockers, the Throne Room's Antechamber lever, the buttons behind the Closed Exhibit. Closed Exhibit placed directly south of the Garage → run back in for the crown without the timer extension. The Boiler Room elevator can be lowered, but it clips you through the floor and costs the day.

Mora Jai boxes

Tile rules, then the answers

3×3 grid, four corner buttons. Get the realm's colour into all four corners, then press the sigils. Pressing corners on a wrong state resets, that's your undo.

Tile rules
Tile On press
Grey Nothing.
White Flips itself and orthogonal neighbours between white and grey. No adjacent greys → it goes grey.
Yellow Swaps with the tile above. Top row → nothing.
Purple / Violet Swaps with the tile below. Bottom row → nothing.
Green Swaps with its 180° mirror through centre. Centre → nothing.
Pink Rotates all 8 surrounding tiles one step clockwise.
Orange Becomes the majority colour of orthogonal neighbours. Tie → nothing.
Black Shifts its whole row one right.
Red All white → black, all black → red. Destructive; last resort.
Blue Copies the centre tile's ability.

Bingo

Steering the pool toward many objectives

The levers, ranked

  1. Duct draws. Uniform across 13 rooms, rarity ignored. Activated Boiler Room → 70% first, 30% after, 3 per draft. The best Rare engine in the game.
  2. Shrink the pool. The gate marks small decks then picks uniformly among them. Library, Prism Key, Secret Passage, corner drafting, many redraws.
  3. Library for Foundation / Study / Attic, the only consistent way, ~50:50 Unusual/Rare. Remember it gives free rooms only at 0 gems.
  4. Silver Key on an edge for 3-ways and the Pool. Hold 4+ gems or it prefers gemless.
  5. Secret Passage, free gem room in slot 1, and a filter wipe so the next draft starts clean.
  6. Burn two free drafts to unlock the gem path.

Reading the board mid-draft

  • A room you don't own two of, shown twice → that rarity's deck is depleted. Stop redrawing.
  • An unexplained Closet → attempt 4 fired. Nothing passed filters.
  • Early Utility Closet / Garage / Billiard → V-Mode is on.
  • Two dead ends + a gem room walling you in → validation's Any Draw crossing both groups. Expected.

Archives, reading the hidden room

Ways to identify what the Archives is hiding:

  • Gem cost is visible.
  • Security door opened with a Keycard reveals the stated rarity.
  • Normal draws are sorted by gem cost, then stated rarity.
  • Active steam power → blue line if the room has a duct.
  • Outer room positions 9–16 repeat 1–8.
  • The three rooms can't normally all be dead ends.
  • Furnace heat appears and stays once a red room is drawn.

Case studies

"What would duck do?"

1 · Corridor over anything, don't waste doors early

Answer: Corridor. A Corridor gives no resource, but saving doors in the pool is the resource. Wasting one early means more dead ends in every one of the 30+ remaining drafts. Approach Day One expecting to draft every Common eventually, so take the one that keeps the path open.

2 · Billiard Room over Commissary at rank 1

Answer: Billiard Room. Not Commissary, no gold, and it'd sit far from the backtrack path. Not Bedroom, that benefits from being near backtrack routes, and you want to fish for Hammers first. Once the dart is solved the Billiard Room can be ignored, so parking it at rank 1 is fine.

3 · Don't skip the first Garage

Answer: Garage. Rule of thumb, never skip the first Garage offered. It has a 90% bonus appearance rate until first shown, then the offer expires. It's crucial for reaching the Outer Room, and 3 keys for 1 gem is good on its own.

4 · At B1/D1, explore the north door first

Answer: the North door to B2. Because the game can't normally offer three dead ends, the Draftable Area of the west door to A1 always contains A2. So check north first: if B2 paths to A2, put a dead end at A1. If not, put a bend at A1 and reach A2 from there.

5 · Normal key or Silver Key at a locked Chapel door?

Answer: Silver Key. Silver doesn't consume the Garage forced draw (unlike SGK), so you can still find the Garage at A7/A8. Garage at A6 isn't bad, but a 3-way is better, it gets you two more rooms. And holding 4+ gems with enough gem 3-ways already drafted (Aquarium, Courtyard, Security), you're likely to find the Pool, which is what the Silver Key is for.

6 · Secret Passage into greens on an East edge

Answer: Secret Passage → green. Advancing on the East edge, green Secret Passage has two different high rolls: a free Veranda, or a Morning Room without needing to buy Bacon from the Kitchen.

7 · Which Outer Room, with Power Hammer already?

Answer: Tomb. Power Hammer already gives Gearside. Antechamber is very likely via Weight Room (Power Hammer + Furnace). So what's missing is Cartside, and with no Silver Key or dice, Tomb is the answer. Trading Post is second (could trade into Silver Key). Shrine is a surprising third, on Berry Picker.

8 · At Underground Gear with the Power Hammer, what before you leave?

Answer: pick up Mechanarium, and leave the gear on this setting. You still need Cartside and Antechamber; Mechanarium solves both, since the 6th door gives a Silver Key for finding the Pool. Picking up Mechanarium is objectively good play in most games. Leaving the gear set correctly lets you reach the Inner Sanctum directly from Cartside.

9 · When to reroll instead of taking a room

Answer: reroll. With Tomb and a lowered Foundation you already hold both Underground sides. What you want is a route to the Secret Garden Key (Locksmith / Music Room), Great Hall, or Workshop. Pool gives no branch and Pump Room has no value here, skip both.

10 · Open the trunk now, or wait for a Hammer?

Answer: open now. No coin to buy a Hammer at the Commissary means no good likelihood of finding one soon. The trunk offers real outs, 2 keys, 3 keys, 2 dice, Lockpick, Keycard. And the Nook is far enough that returning later with a Hammer is too expensive step-wise, especially with no early Nursery or Sleeping Mask.

Room 46

You made it

Reaching it once permanently changes the engine: the gem deck gate locks to 5/5/4/4, rotation reverts to the Default column, the Trophy Room unblocks, and your next draft hands you a Gift Shop in Slot 1 whether you want one or not.
